I keep myself pretty busy - after programming, the other hobbies I indulge are reading and (occassionally) writing.
You can't learn how to code except by actually coding, and you can't understand something unless you're able to describe it in regular language. Hence, my tech blog. I only post there when I've completed a significant hobby-project.
Also, I'm trying to fill in the gaps in my education. My thinking is very traditional and old-fashioned - I believe university graduates should have a solid liberal education in addition to their specialties. Computer science and mathematics are beautiful subjects for me, but I pretty much skipped the humanities while getting my BSCS. Hence, I'm reading through the 50-volume Harvard Classics and keeping a blog to spur me on and share with my bibliophile friends.
